A complete guide to choosing the right controller for Ableton Live.  


If you're looking for the **best Ableton Live controller in 2026**, you've probably seen dozens of options: Ableton Push 3, Ableton Move, TouchAble, LK, Knobber and new iPad/iPhone apps like **7III Tap**.

This guide compares the most popular controllers so you can choose the one that fits your workflow. Whether you're performing live, producing in the studio, or controlling Ableton on the road. We have only included devices that maximize Ableton Live integration, so this comparison omits devices like the Novation Launchpad, which is part of a family of more universal controllers that are missing important Ableton Live features.

## Controller Breakdown

### Hardware Controllers
- Sturdy, but also heavy
- Have to use hands, not fingers
- Expensive

#### Push 3 ‚Äî The Industry Standard
Ableton's flagship controller offers:
- deep integration  
- exceptional build quality  
- expressive pads with MPE 
- standalone mode (optional)  

Cons:
- limited customization  
- heavy
- the sequencer sucks

Best for: **studio musicians and producers that want hardware pads.**

Website: [ableton.com/en/push](https://www.ableton.com/en/push)


#### Move ‚Äî Grooving on the Go
A compact and modern groove box with:
- solid pads with Aftertouch  
- USB connectivity  
- Ableton Live in a small package
- cool groove box, perfect for sampling

Cons:
- small screen
- not the best sequencer
- limited customization  
- no MPE

Best for: **producers who want a hardware feel without the size of Push.**

Website: [ableton.com/en/move/](https://www.ableton.com/en/move/)

### Touchscreen Controllers
- much cheaper than hardware if you already own an iPhone or iPad
- lack of pressure sensitivity, aftertouch etc. -> if you are a piano nerd or finger drumming expert you might want to use extra hardware pads or a keyboard.

#### TouchAble ‚Äî Deep Integration without Support
TouchAble is a deeply integrated and powerful controller on iPad:
- Control almost anything in Ableton Live

Cons:
- no updates  
- UI looks pretty bad 
- not very performance-focused
- expensive for a mobile app
- needs a server to connect

Website: [zerodebug.com](https://zerodebug.com)

#### Tap ‚Äî Max Performance App
- Best in class step sequencer, no matter if you make a crazy beat or add beautiful harmonies, with amazing tools and creative MIDI modulations.
- 8 finger parameter encoder editing and the before mentioned step sequencer or MIDI pads or keyboards in the device view.
- Everything is made for (live) performance: Quick channel and view switching. Great mixer with clip launcher, amazing clip launcher with device control and encoders.
- Plug in and play (needs a remote script)
- extra customizable universal MIDI encoders pages.

Cons: 
- No custom pages for devices yet
- No Android app
 
Tap is the best choice for:
- **mobile producers**  
- **live performers on iPad**  
- **iPhone users**  
- anyone who wants Push-like workflows without hardware  

Website: [project7iii.com/tap](/tap)



#### Knobber ‚Äî Tweak those Parameters
A compact controller with:
- deeply integrated parameter controls 
- custom layouts  

Cons:
- no keyboards
- no sequencers
- no clips
- needs a Max for Live device
- expensive for a mobile app

Best for: **minimal controllers or simple parameter control.**

Website: [plugins.steinkamp.us/m4l-Knobbler4](https://plugins.steinkamp.us/m4l-Knobbler4)


#### LK ‚Äî Universal MIDI Controller App
LK offers:
- clip launching  
- mixing
- basic sequencing  
- Universal MIDI Control and sequencing

Cons:
- Ugly UI


Best for: **Controlling MIDI devices** without Ableton Live

Website: [imaginando.pt/products/lk-ableton-live-and-midi-controller](https://www.imaginando.pt/products/lk-ableton-live-and-midi-controller)


## Which Controller Should You Choose?

### Choose **Push 3** if:  
You want the deepest hardware integration and best pads.

### Choose **Move** if:  
You want an amazing small groove box with portability and a small controller as a bonus.

### Choose **Tap** if:  
You want the **best mobile Ableton controller** for:
- rock solid live performances
- deep sequencing with tools
- playing Ableton Live like an instrument
- clean interface  

### Choose **LK** if:  
You want to control MIDI devices without Ableton Live.

### Choose **Knobber** if:  
All you need are parameter controls.
